Impact Investing

Impact investing is the direction of capital into companies, organisations or funds with the intention of generating beneficial and measurable social or environmental impact along with financial returns. It focuses on utilising a venture building approach to grow scalable business models that address core challenges of the society like financial inclusion, clean energy, education, sanitation, healthcare etc. Investing to create measurable social or environmental impact is more relevant than ever in today’s context of COVID-19 which has strongly affected poor and marginalised. It has become more critical than ever to step up and invest resources in rebuilding our communities and society.
Today impact investors aren’t just high net worth families, foundations, pension funds or insurance companies. As per The India Impact Investing Story, IIC and Asha Impact, June 2020, approximately $3.5 billion out of the $10.8 billion invested over the last decade, came from mainstream Private equity (PE) and venture capital (VC) investors mostly in the last few years. There was a dramatic improvement in the amount of funding from the impact investors from $860 million in 2010-12 to $4.9 billion in 2016-19, representative of their commitment. Impact investments have contributed to 11 of 17 SDGs and has the potential to unlock part of the $600 billion of additional private capital needed each year to achieve India’s SDG targets.

Social enterprises benefit from impact investments

In the last decade, Impact investment has made meaningful contributions to our country’s development. It has provided a high degree of innovation and market-based scalability with a relatively modest amount, which has helped outsize impact in terms of human lives in India. In the past decade, equity capital of $10.8 billion flowed in at a CAGR of 26%, with a lot of the growth concentrated in the past two years. For each dollar of funding, at least twice as much commercial capital and impact investors have been brought in through impact investments. They have also helped enterprises scale their operations further by bringing in seed and Series-A capital. Impact investing has impacted ~200 million people through improved service delivery for better accessibility and reach across different sectors primarily in the sectors of health, education, agriculture and financial services and another 300 million people through the strategic use of technology for development. They have managed to achieve this by driving business model and technology innovation that is focused on service delivery at the last mile in a way that is financially sustainable and scalable.

Our Objective through Impact Pitch Deck

Through the Social Enterprise conclave and the Impact Pitch Deck competition, we envisage bringing together the important stakeholders of this ecosystem i.e. the social entrepreneurs and investor such as Government, Private Equity players, Venture Capitalist, HNIs, Private Organizations and others. The competition aims to provide a platform for the social enterprises to pitch their ideas in front of the investors, corporates, accelerators and media to harness support for growth. The process involves filling the general entry form with basic details and our team shall get in touch with you to submit the entries in defined format. The entries shall be then evaluated by our esteemed panel of jury and selected social enterprises shall be selected for further rounds of presentation.

About US

The Confederation of Indian Industry (CII) works to create and sustain an environment conducive to the development of India, partnering industry, Government and civil society, through advisory and consultative processes. For 125 years, CII has been working on shaping India's development journey and, this year, more than ever before, it will continue to proactively transform Indian industry's engagement in national development. CII is a non-government, not-for-profit, industry-led and industry-managed organization, with about 9100 members from the private as well as public sectors, including SMEs and MNCs, and an indirect membership of over 300,000 enterprises from 288 national and regional sectoral industry bodies. CII charts change by working closely with Government on policy issues, interfacing with thought leaders, and enhancing efficiency, competitiveness and business opportunities for industry through a range of specialized services and strategic global linkages. It also provides a platform for consensus-building and networking on key issues. Extending its agenda beyond business, CII assists industry to identify and execute corporate citizenship programmes. Partnerships with civil society organizations carry forward corporate initiatives for integrated and inclusive development across diverse domains including affirmative action, livelihoods, diversity management, skill development, empowerment of women, and sustainable development, to name a few. With the Theme for 2020-21 as Building India for a New World: Lives, Livelihood, Growth, CII will work with Government and industry to bring back growth to the economy and mitigate the enormous human cost of the pandemic by protecting jobs and livelihoods. With 68 offices, including 10 Centres of Excellence, in India, and 8 overseas offices in Australia, Egypt, Germany, Indonesia, Singapore, UAE, UK, and USA, as well as institutional partnerships with 394 counterpart organizations in 133 countries, CII serves as a reference point for Indian industry and the international business community.
